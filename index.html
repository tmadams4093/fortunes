Explanation

Shakespeare loves using dramatic irony in his plays, and Othello is no exception. There are many instances of this throughout the play. All of Iago's soliloquies are an example of this, as the audience knows his schemes while the characters do not.

Desdemona singing the willow song is another moment of dramatic irony. She's foreshadowing her own death, and since it is a tragedy, the audience knows that she will die.

This fortune teller attraction showcases the dramatic irony throughout the play, with fortunes relating to the character's fate.
